「声学」adiabtic law

Apr 24, 2018 | 学习

The speed of sound in air

For ideal gases Poisson’s law appplies as a state equation.(adiabatic process)
$$\frac{p_{g}}{p_{0}}=\left ( \frac{\rho_{g}}{\rho_{0}} \right )^{\kappa }$$

下标g表示gas
这个一个压力和密度的关系公式
The relationship between pressure and density in a sound wave runs adiabatically due to the rapid changes. That is, without the heat exchange between adjacent volume elements.
adiabatic exponent $\kappa =1.4$ for air.and from that:
$$c^{2}=\left ( \frac{dp_{g}}{d\rho_{g}} \right )_{ad}=\frac{\kappa p_g}{\rho_g}$$
When $p\ll p_0$, $\rho \ll \rho_0$ and $v$ is very small, then $\rho_g$ can be replaced by $\rho_0$.
for small fluctuation,压力和总压力相比很小的时候，我们可以得到$\tilde{p}=c^{2}\tilde{\rho}$

According to the general gas equation it is
$$p_{g}=\frac{\rho_{g}\tilde{R}\theta }{M^{‘}}$$
absolute temperature in $K$, $\theta$
universal gas constant, $\tilde{R}=8,31 J/K \cdot mol.$
molecular weight in $kg/mol$, $M^{‘}$
with this,
$$c=\sqrt{\frac{\kappa \tilde{R}\theta }{M^{‘}}}$$
Especially for air

$c=331.4+0.6\vartheta m/s$

is a good approximation for many practical applications (temperature in degree Celsius, $\vartheta$).

the speed of sound in liquids

Liquids can be better characterized by applying the adiabatic compressibility
$$\beta_{ad}=\frac{1}{\rho_{0}}\left (\frac{d\rho_{g}}{dp_{g}} \right )_{ad}$$

from which follows
$$c=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\rho_{0}\beta_{ad}}}$$
